Why this shape

A single red bar overstates the situation. It correctly finds the worst limit, but hides that another subscription is available.

A blended percentage would understate it. Averaging 98% and 10% into 54% suggests Claude/Fable is usable when it is not.

Separate mini-meters preserve scope. Red remains attached to the constrained pool; blue remains attached to the healthy one. The shared shell avoids turning every account into a full topbar chip.

Summary text describes availability: “1 constrained · 1 healthy.” It never claims all quota is critical unless every usable pool is critical.

Decision in one glance

The proposal borrows the separate-signal behavior of machine chips without paying the full horizontal cost per account.
