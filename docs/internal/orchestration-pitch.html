Podium Orchestration

You describe the goal.
Your agents keep working.

Podium turns a wish into tracked work, runs it across your coding agents, and only interrupts you when your judgment is genuinely needed — with everything you need to decide in one card.

Start with a wish

Everything starts at the + button. No forms, no picking the right board — just say it. The concierge finds or creates the right epic, breaks it into issues with dependencies, and kicks off the first agents. And while the system works, it's someone to talk to: ask "where is dark mode?" and get an answer, not a wall of terminals.

Issues are the shared memory

Every piece of work is an issue with dependencies — the same tracker you use, drivable by agents. Agents claim work, file what they discover, and leave a completion note when they finish: a short handoff that becomes the message the next agent (or you) reads. Close a blocker and everything waiting on it becomes ready — automatically.

Nothing lives in anyone's head.
Sessions crash, contexts fill up, days end. The issue graph is the durable state of the work — any agent can pick it up cold.
The board never lies.
A housekeeping service (the steward) reconciles reality with the tracker: stages, titles, completion notes, discovered work — and nudges agents that forgot their paperwork.

The pump — a thermostat, not another AI

The pump is the part that keeps work flowing when you're not looking. It's important to understand what it is: a deterministic rule, not an agent. It never thinks, never plans, never spends a single token itself.

Like a thermostat: it doesn't decide what temperature is right — you set that per epic. It just switches the heater on and off. The only thing the pump ever "costs" is the agent sessions it starts — which run on the subscriptions you already have, and which it will never start beyond your budget.

You choose how autonomous each epic is

Autonomy is a setting on the epic, not a global switch. Different work, different trust.

Default

Interactive

  • Nothing starts unless you start it
  • Agent questions come to you
  • You merge
  • Autonomous spend: zero. Exactly today's behavior, plus the bookkeeping.
Keep it moving

Auto

  • Pump starts ready work — within your budget & caps
  • Agent questions still come to you, as decision cards
  • You confirm merges
  • Spend: bounded by the budget you set on this epic.
Overnight mode

Full Autonomy

  • Pump starts ready work within budget
  • Agents decide routine questions and record why in their notes
  • Merges when checks are green
  • Hard failures still stop and wait for you
  • Spend: same bound. "Full" means fewer interruptions — never unlimited cost.

What it costs — no surprises

Orchestration doesn't add a new meter. It decides when your existing agents run — and gives you hard stops.

The pump & bookkeeping

Concierge chat & decision cards
intake, briefs, judgment calls		 Small calls on the background model you configure (a cheap/fast one by default; your subscription or key) cents
budget per epic

A hard stop, not a suggestion. When it's spent, the pump stops starting work on that epic and tells you.

concurrency caps

Max auto-started sessions per repo and per machine — also respects your agents' own rate-limit gauges.

pause

One switch freezes all autonomous dispatch, everywhere, instantly. Running agents finish; nothing new starts.

audit trail

Every autonomous action — every dispatch, nudge, merge — is a comment on the issue and a row in the activity feed. Nothing happens silently.

Rule of thumb: Interactive costs what Podium costs you today. Auto/Full cost what you'd have spent running those same agents by hand — minus the hours where work sat idle waiting for you to notice a finished blocker.

Come back without the whiplash

The most expensive thing in agent work isn't tokens — it's your context switch. When an agent genuinely needs you, Podium doesn't hand you a terminal scrollback. It hands you a decision card.
